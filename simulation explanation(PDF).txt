\section{Simulation Explanation and Analysis}

The simulation was developed using GlowScript VPython to model the motion of a skydiver under the influence of gravity and air resistance. It visually demonstrates how terminal velocity is reached and how the deployment of a parachute affects the motion.

\subsection{Initial Setup}

At the start of the simulation, the skydiver is positioned at a certain height above the ground with an initial velocity of zero. The only significant force acting at this moment is gravity, which initiates the downward motion.

The simulation includes a 3D representation of the skydiver, a ground surface, and a parachute that becomes visible during the fall.
\begin{bluebox}
    
}
 \subsection{Stage 1: Free Fall}

In the initial stage, the skydiver is in free fall. Gravity is the dominant force, and air resistance is very small due to the low velocity.

As a result:
\begin{itemize}
    \item The acceleration is approximately equal to $g$
    \item The velocity increases rapidly
\end{itemize}

This stage is clearly observed in the simulation as a rapid increase in speed.
\end{bluebox}
\begin{orangebox}
    

\subsection{Stage 2: Increasing Air Resistance and Terminal Velocity}

As the skydiver continues to fall, the velocity increases, and air resistance becomes more significant. The drag force opposes the motion and reduces the net force acting on the body.

During this stage:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Acceleration gradually decreases
    \item Velocity continues to increase, but at a slower rate
\end{itemize}

Eventually, the drag force becomes equal to the gravitational force. At this point:
\[
F_g = F_{\text{drag}}
\]

and the net force becomes zero. This results in zero acceleration, and the skydiver reaches terminal velocity.

In the simulation, this is observed when the velocity values become nearly constant.
\end{orangebox}
\begin{bluebox}
    

\subsection{Stage 3: Parachute Deployment and Controlled Descent}

At a specific time, the parachute is deployed. This is represented visually by the appearance of a parachute above the skydiver.

The deployment causes a sudden increase in surface area, which greatly increases air resistance. As a result:
\begin{itemize}
    \item The drag force temporarily exceeds the gravitational force
    \item The skydiver decelerates (slows down)
\end{itemize}

After a short period, a new balance between forces is achieved, and the skydiver reaches a second, lower terminal velocity. This ensures a safe and controlled descent.
\end{bluebox}
